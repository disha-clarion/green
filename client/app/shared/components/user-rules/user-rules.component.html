Parameters
  • Nominal Compression recommended for 20-25% at operating temperature.

    Depending on the operating temperature, ambient compression can be lower(but not below 15%), but with risk of sealing at ambient.

    Higher compression than 25% can result in reduced lifetime of product.

  • Nominal Gland Fill recommended for 85-90% at operating temperature.

    Higher gland fill can result in reduced lifetime of product. As gland fill reaches 100% premature failure of the o-ring increases

    Lower gland fill is acceptable, however gland fill improves the sealing force of the o-ring

  • Nominal Stretch recommended is 1- 3% at ambient

    Higher stretch can result in reduced lifetime of product

    Less than 1% stretch is acceptable for o-rings under 1" in diameter.

    Stretch is required to account for o-ring tolerances and prevent bunching up of the o-ring in the groove

    Stretch is not calculated at operating temperature. It is encapsulated by the gland, which makes gland fill and compression calculations important for improved lifetime.

  • Nominal Radial Compression recommended is 0%
Gland Type
  • Rectangular Glands

    It is recommended for the gland width to be larger than the cross section of the o-ring

    One Dimension to the ID or OD of the gland only, and then dimension the gland width. Otherwise total tolerance stackup is too large and causes problems

    • This enables the ID or OD tolerance to only affect stretch or radial compression
    • This enables the gland width tolerance to only affect gland fill
  • Dovetail Glands

    Use the centerline of the gland for machining and stretch calculations

    Always call out a top radius. No top radius callout has resulted in cut o-rings during o-ring installation

    Dovetail Gland Angle recommended 55-66 degrees

    • Use 66 degrees for ambient up to approximately 140C
    • Use 55 degrees for temperatures above approximately 140C
    • 45 degree angles may have to be used for very small cross section
  • Half Dovetail Glands

    Greene Tweed does not typically recommend the use of these glands due to very high gland fill, which leads to premature seal failures

    • For large chamber seals, the Greene Tweed half dovetail gland design allows for thermal expansion and could be used for easier installation compared to standard rectangular glands
    • Stretch is calculated similar to full dovetail glands via centerline
    • High Gland Fill is usually the result as there is not enough room for a full dovetail gland
    • Only stretch is used to hold the seal in place during installation. If the tool is taken down and the o-ring wiped clean, it may not be reusable as the seal has likely grown slightly and therefore stretch may not exists to hold the seal during re installation
  • Minimum radii is .005" due to typical machining standards though sharp edges can occur on dovetail grooves if not called out
O-ring Sizing
  • All tolerances are based upon AS568A sizes

    No tolerance changes allowed

    Custom o-ring sizes can result in higher tolerances

  • O-ring Cross Sections

    .070 and .103 (-000 and -100 sizes) cross sections not recommended for high temperatures

    .139 (-200 size) cross sections will have sealing limitations as ambient as temperatures increase past 200C. Greene Tweed does offer custom seal designs that can seal both at 300C and at ambient temperatures.

    .210 and .275 (-300 and -400 sizes) cross sections recommended for highest temperatures when the user wants to use standard o-ring sizes

  • Each material has a maximum temperature limit.
